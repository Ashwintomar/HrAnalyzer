What this project demonstrates clearly
  • AI support makes repetitive HR operations substantially more manageable under high applicant volume.
  • Recruiters perceive value fastest when automation removes intake friction and top-of-funnel overload.
  • Resume-semantic ranking is useful for triage but insufficient for final candidate quality judgment.
  • Interview-support generation is a practical, lower-risk entry point for AI adoption in HR workflows.
What this project does not claim
  • It does not claim that AI should replace HR decision-makers.
  • It does not claim universal fairness from semantic ranking alone.
  • It does not claim that polished resumes are equivalent to verified candidate competence.
  • It does not claim causal hiring-quality uplift from a limited pilot without broader validation.

Governance Design

The control layers recommended by the report for responsible deployment in India-focused HR workflows.

Data layer

Retention and consent discipline

Keep candidate records purpose-limited, auditable, and aligned with retention expectations.

Model layer

Rank quality monitoring

Track drift, edge cases, and false-confidence patterns instead of treating rankings as static truth.

Decision layer

Mandatory human override

Require explicit reviewer validation for shortlist approval, escalation, and final selection logic.

Organization layer

Reviewer training

Teach teams the limits of AI triage so operational speed does not quietly outrun judgment quality.
